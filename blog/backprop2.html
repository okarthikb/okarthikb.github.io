What is a neural net, anyway?

A neural net is just a complex function whose parameters we can adjust to fit some data. The input and output of a neural network are vectors, which are just lists of numbers. The input can be say, four numbers (a vector of size four) representing blood cholestrol level, oxygen level, etc., of a person, and the output may be a single number between 0 and 1 predicting the risk of heart disease (or something). Our goal is to take a bunch of input-output vector pairs (datapoints), and adjust the neural nets parameters to fit the datapoints. Let's see the anatomy of a neural net.


The perceptron

The perceptron is the basic unit of a neural net. The perceptron represents the following function - take input numbers (a vector), scales each by a scalar, adds a bias, then apply a non-linearity.

\(o=\varphi(\sum_{i=1}^nw_ix_i+b)\)

\(w_i\) and \(b\) (the weights and bias) are parameters of the perceptron that we can adjust.
